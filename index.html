Mobile Phone Use Enforcement in Australia

How detection methods and enforcement changed from 2008–2024

@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Background

For a long time, Australia has had a problem with people using mobile phones while driving. Until 2020, the main way to catch offenders was for police officers to spot them visually, which was difficult and limited by their numbers and what they could see.

This changed in 2020 with the introduction of AI-powered detection cameras. This new technology can spot phone use much more accurately and consistently, day and night. This has been a game-changer for enforcement, especially in states like New South Wales.

Trend in Enforcement Over Time

  • Camera-detected fines increased sharply after 2020.
  • Police-issued fines dropped over the same period.
  • Shift from manual to AI-based detection methods.
@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Key Findings

  • In 2024, enforcement totals were higher than in 2023, with camera enforcement leading the charge.
  • New South Wales and Queensland recorded the highest number of fines in 2024
  • Australian Capital Territory had a sharp increment of annual fines since 2023.
  • Northern Territory and Tasmania had the lowest enforcement numbers overall.
@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Effectiveness of Enforcement Methods

  • ACT and NSW have the highest fines per 10,000 drivers.
  • Earlier and wider camera deployment in these states.
  • Camera enforcement appears more effective than police-only.
