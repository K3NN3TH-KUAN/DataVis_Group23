Mobile Phone Use Enforcement in Australia

How detection methods and enforcement changed from 2008–2024

@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Team Member: Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Background

Mobile phone use while driving is a major road safety concern in Australia.

Before 2020, enforcement mainly relied on police officers observing drivers on the road.
From 2020 onwards, several states introduced AI-enabled mobile detection cameras, which significantly increased the number of fines issued — especially in New South Wales.

@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Team Member: Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Trend in Enforcement Over Time

  • Camera-detected fines increased sharply after 2020.
  • Police-issued fines dropped over the same period.
  • Shift from manual to AI-based detection methods.
@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Team Member: Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Enforcement Strength by State

  • NSW and QLD enforce mobile phone laws the most.
  • ACT shows high enforcement relative to peers.
  • TAS, NT, and WA trend lower across years.
@COS30045 Data Visualisation Project Team Member: Voong Zhe Hong, Alvin, Kenneth

Effectiveness of Enforcement Methods

  • ACT and NSW have the highest fines per 10,000 drivers.
  • Earlier and wider camera deployment in these states.
  • Camera enforcement appears more effective than police-only.
